Why does it matter where the beans come from?

Fair trade coffee plays a vital role in promoting ethical and sustainable practices within the global coffee industry. By ensuring that farmers receive a fair price for their crops, fair trade helps protect small-scale growers from exploitation and the volatile fluctuations of the global market. This pricing model not only supports basic living standards but also enables farmers to invest in better farming practices, education, and community development.

Why coffee is good for the microbiome

Coffee has been shown to have positive effects on the gut microbiome, largely due to its rich content of polyphenols and dietary fiber. These compounds serve as prebiotics, which help nourish beneficial gut bacteria such as *Bifidobacteria* and *Lactobacillus*. Studies suggest that regular coffee consumption may increase microbial diversity, a key marker of gut health, and reduce the abundance of harmful bacteria linked to inflammation. Additionally, coffee's antioxidant properties may support the gut lining and contribute to overall digestive health. As a result, moderate coffee intake can be a valuable part of a gut-friendly diet.
